Teaching Philosophy

Teaching represents the most meaningful intersection of my professional practice and commitment to interior design knowledge. The act of educating another person is a privilege that requires intentional preparation, thoughtful engagement, and a responsibility to foster both intellectual and professional growth. I am particularly motivated by moments when students gain clarity and confidence as they connect conceptual ideas to practical application. This motivation informs my teaching approach, which emphasizes scaffolded learning, experiential engagement, and the intentional integration of theory with professional practice.

I develop students through a scaffolded learning process that begins with foundational design principles and progresses toward advanced application in pragmatic, real-world scenarios. My goal is to help students understand how abstract concepts are realized in the built environment and how design decisions directly affect the people who inhabit these spaces. Students are encouraged not only to master form and function, but also to recognize how thoughtful design can positively influence human interaction, well-being, and experience. Complex ideas are intentionally broken into manageable components that build upon one another throughout the curriculum and are reinforced through site visits, precedent studies, cultural context, and reflection on lived experience.

I believe interior design education must be approached holistically. While students must develop a strong foundation in the elements and principles of design, they should also be guided in cultivating professional competencies such as time management, effective client communication, pragmatic problem-solving, and working within established budgets. These skills are reinforced through iterative feedback, design critiques, and evaluation methods that emphasize growth, accountability, and clarity of communication. I also encourage students to engage early with local chapters of national design organizations to develop professional responsibility, ethical awareness, and a commitment to lifelong learning beyond the classroom.

As an educator, I view teaching as both a responsibility and a form of service, grounded in a deep sense of joy and purpose. I am committed to fostering a supportive learning environment where students feel encouraged to ask questions, explore ideas, and develop confidence in their abilities. Through mentorship, clear expectations, and an emphasis on integrity and professionalism, I aim to prepare students not only for successful careers in interior design, but also for thoughtful engagement with the communities and environments they will ultimately serve.
