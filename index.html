About

Laura Harris is a Registered Interior Designer (RID) with over fifteen years of professional interior design experience spanning commercial, institutional, and residential work. She holds a Bachelor of Fine Arts in Interior Design from Samford University, whose undergraduate program is accredited by the Council for Interior Design Accreditation (CIDA), and a Master of Fine Arts in Interior Design from Brenau University.

Her graduate research focused on the relationship between educational facility design and adolescent social development. This research continues to inform her interest in human-centered design, evidence-based planning, and the role of interior environments in shaping social experience.

Award

Awarded the ASID Foundation's Joel Polsky Academic Achievement Award in recognition of her thesis findings.

Laura is a LEED Accredited Professional (ID+C), NCIDQ certified, and a Registered Interior Designer in the state of Alabama. She is a Past President of the International Interior Design Association (IIDA) Alabama Chapter and remains active with the Alabama Interior Design Coalition, supporting professional advocacy and continuing education in the design community.

She is currently employed with Will & Pierce Agency, where she educates architects and interior designers on commercial plumbing products and delivers accredited continuing education courses throughout Alabama, Mississippi, and Northwest Florida. She has presented more than seventy-five CEU programs on accessibility, sustainability, and hygienic design considerations. She also maintains an active small-scale residential practice focused on thoughtful renovation and livable design.
