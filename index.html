About

Laura Harris is a registered interior designer with over nine years of commercial interior design experience. She graduated with a Bachelor of Fine Arts in Interior Design from Samford University, which offers an undergraduate interior design program accredited by the Council for Interior Design Accreditation (CIDA). In 2009, she passed the Leadership in Energy and Environment Design (LEED) exam. She also passed the National Council for Interior Design Qualification (NCIDQ) examination in 2012 and became a registered interior designer in the state of Alabama. She is a volunteer on the board of the International Interior Design Association (IIDA) Alabama Chapter, which coordinates professional events for the interior design community including joint ventures with the American Society of Interior Designers (ASID) and the Alabama Interior Design Coalition (AIDC).

Award

Received her Master of Fine Arts in Interior Design from Brenau University in May 2019 and was awarded the Joel Polsky Academic Achievement Award offered by ASID for her thesis findings.

Laura is currently employed by Capstone Real Estate Investments which provides design and procurement services for student housing facilities across the country. She works closely with the company's developer partners as well as university management and owners to create functional furniture plans, select high quality furnishings, coordinate fabrics and interior finishes, facilitate purchasing, and manage the installation process. She hopes to expand her career by utilizing her graduate degree to instruct the next generation of interior designers in addition to her current responsibilities.
