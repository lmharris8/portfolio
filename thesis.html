Designing Educational Facilities for Social Engagement in Adolescents

Master of Fine Arts Thesis — Brenau University, 2019

Joel Polsky Academic Achievement Award — ASID

Purpose

Research indicates that social development is important for the health of adolescents, suggesting a need for additional interior design methods in educational facility planning that encourage student social engagement. The purpose of this study is to determine the degree to which middle school educators perceive the built environment of secondary educational facilities as influencing the social development of adolescents.

Methodology

A qualitative research study utilizing a case study methodology was established to delve into educator perceptions regarding the correlation between student social engagement and educational facility design. Nine educators from seven educational facilities participated in the research sample. All educators taught seventh through ninth grade near Birmingham, Alabama. Data was collected through a combination of interviews and site visits, then analyzed using a general inductive approach.

Key Findings

Three significant findings emerged from the research:

1

Built Environment Matters

All participants indicated that the built environment of an educational facility is important to student social development.

2

Five Design Elements

Participants identified five elements that influence social development: space and ceiling heights, lighting and windows, color and visual complexity, furniture, and acoustics.

3

Six Facility Areas

Participants identified six areas that impact social development: classrooms, outdoor spaces, hallways and locker areas, libraries and theaters, gymnasiums, and auxiliary meeting spaces.

Implications

It is intended that these findings will lead to more effective educational design practices that benefit adolescent students and the interior design community. The research provides actionable insights for designers, educators, and facility planners working to create environments that support the social well-being of young people.

Studio Application

The research findings informed the design of an educational facility that demonstrates how evidence-based interior design strategies can support adolescent social development.
